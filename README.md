# 📊 AURORA SOUND FEST 2025 ANALYSIS  

The main objective of this project is to analyze the performance of Aurora Sound Fest 2025 in terms of attendance, costs, music genres, vendor activity, and total revenue. Additionally, a simple growth model is used to forecast the festival's potential revenue for future years.

#### Important:
All data used in this project is fictional and was generated specifically for educational and portfolio purposes. The project was built from scratch using Python to generate the datasets, SQLite for data storage and analysis, and Power BI for visualization and dashboard development.

#### Dataset Creation

The following datasets were generated in Python:

#### Artists

A list of twenty fictional artists was created, along with six music genres and six countries. Through random assignment, each artist received:

An Artist ID
A Genre
A Country of Origin
A Performance Fee

#### Performances

Festival performance schedules were generated using:

Three festival dates
Performance start times
Performance durations

Each performance was randomly assigned to an artist and given a unique Performance ID.

#### Sponsors

Five fictional sponsors were created. Each sponsor received:

A Sponsor ID
A Contribution Amount

#### Stages

Three festival stages were created. Each stage was assigned:

A Stage ID
A Stage Name
A Capacity

#### Ticket Types

Three ticket categories were created:

General
VIP
Platinum

Each ticket type was assigned a price and a unique Ticket Type ID.

#### Ticket Sales

The ticket sales dataset contains:

Purchase Date
Customer City
Customer Age
Ticket Type
Performance ID

Ticket records were generated by randomly assigning customers to performances and ticket categories across fifteen different cities.

#### Vendors

Twenty vendors (a mix of fictional and real-inspired businesses) were created. Each vendor received:

A Vendor ID
A Category (Food, Drinks, or Merchandise)
A Commission Rate (10%, 15%, or 20%)

#### Vendor Sales

Vendor sales were generated using category-specific price ranges:

Food: 30,000–60,000 COP
Drinks: 15,000–25,000 COP
Merchandise: 50,000–90,000 COP

A quantity sold was randomly assigned, and total sales were calculated by multiplying unit price by quantity sold.

#### Data Analysis Process

After generating the datasets, the information was imported into SQLite, where the data was organized and analyzed through SQL queries to answer business questions related to festival performance.

The cleaned data was then imported into Power BI to create an interactive dashboard and visualize key insights.

#### Currency Note

All monetary values in this project are expressed in Colombian Pesos (COP).

Since the analysis is presented in English, large values are referenced using the English numbering system. For example:

1 billion COP = 1,000,000,000 COP
Equivalent to "mil millones de pesos" in Spanish

Power BI's regional formatting settings display some values using Spanish abbreviations such as "mil M". Therefore, a value displayed as "20 mil M" should be interpreted as approximately 20 billion COP rather than 20 million COP.

* #### Which Genre Generated The Most Revenue?

<p align="center">

As shown in the chart, the genres that generated the highest revenue were Merengue and Pop, which also happen to be the genres represented by the largest number of artists at the festival.

Reggaeton ranked third in terms of revenue generation. A possible explanation is the strong popularity of this genre in Medellín, where reggaeton has played an important role in the local music scene over the last two decades.

The next most profitable genres were Rock and Salsa. The performance of Rock may reflect the long-standing rock culture that has existed in Medellín since the 1980s. Although Salsa generated less than 10 billion COP (Around 2,832,412 USD) in revenue, it still contributed a significant amount, which may be related to the genre's continued popularity across Latin America.

Finally, Alternative generated the lowest revenue among all genres. This may be explained by the fact that only one artist from this genre was included in the festival lineup, as well as the relatively smaller Alternative music audience represented in the dataset.

To estimate the festival's revenue for 2026, a simple growth model was applied using a projected annual increase of 10% over the 2025 results.

Based on the festival's total revenue of more than 83.87 billion COP (approximately 23.77 million USD) in 2025, the forecast suggests that Aurora Sound Fest 2026 could generate over 92.2 billion COP (approximately 26.11 million USD) in total revenue.

Although this projection is based on a simplified growth assumption rather than historical trends, it provides a useful estimate of the festival's future financial performance. If attendance, sponsorship contributions, and vendor activity continue to grow at a similar rate, Aurora Sound Fest could surpass 92 billion COP in revenue during its next edition.


## 📖 Conclusion

Based on the results obtained throughout this analysis, Aurora Sound Fest 2025 can be considered a highly successful event. The festival generated more than 83.87 billion COP (approximately 23.77 million USD) in total revenue, with the vast majority coming from ticket sales, which alone produced over 83.66 billion COP (approximately 23.71 million USD).

The results also suggest that the festival represented a solid investment opportunity. Sponsor contributions totaled approximately 210 million COP (around 59,520 USD), while vendor commissions and on-site sales provided additional revenue streams. Given the overall financial performance, future editions of the festival could potentially achieve even greater success through adjustments such as expanding sponsorship agreements, attracting additional high-profile artists, and optimizing vendor selection.

Several interesting patterns emerged from the analysis. Although Rock was not the highest-revenue genre overall, Rock artists attracted some of the largest audiences, indicating a strong connection between attendees and those performers. Another notable finding was the popularity of Platinum tickets, which generated the highest ticket revenue despite being the most expensive option available. Finally, the strongest attendance levels were observed among middle-aged attendees, suggesting that the festival appealed particularly well to adult audiences.

Overall, the project demonstrates how data analysis tools such as Python, SQLite, and Power BI can be combined to transform raw data into meaningful business insights. Through revenue analysis, attendance trends, customer demographics, vendor performance, and revenue forecasting, Aurora Sound Fest 2025 provides a comprehensive example of how data-driven decision-making can be applied within the live entertainment industry.
